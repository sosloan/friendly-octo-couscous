TEST_HILBERT_PROMPTS — SLED EDITION (State & Local Government)

Mission:
Create playful, practical test prompts for Hilbert-style evaluation focused on SLED workflows,
with special attention to California local operations such as the Santa Clara County Sheriff.

Plan (fun mode: ON):

1) Pick the SLED Personas (the "League of Public Service")
- Sheriff’s Office Dispatcher (Santa Clara County style incident routing)
- Patrol Sergeant (shift planning + response coordination)
- Records Clerk (public records and retention workflows)
- County Emergency Manager (wildfire/flood/citywide alerts)
- City IT Security Lead (CJIS-minded security and audit trails)

2) Build Prompt Themes (the "Civic Questlines")
- Incident triage and priority setting
- Cross-agency coordination (sheriff, fire, EMS, public works)
- Policy-compliant communication templates
- FOIA/public records request handling
- Budget-aware resource allocation
- Community safety messaging in plain language

3) Write Prompt Sets by Difficulty (the "Boss Levels")
- Level 1: Clear single-step asks (status lookup, report summary)
- Level 2: Multi-step operational planning (staffing + response + comms)
- Level 3: Ambiguous crisis scenarios (partial info, time pressure, policy constraints)

4) Add Santa Clara County Sheriff-Flavored Scenarios (without sensitive data)
- Weekend event crowd management near county facilities
- Storm-related road closure coordination across unincorporated areas
- Jail transport scheduling conflicts and escalation paths
- Community outreach plan for neighborhood safety workshops

5) Include Safety + Governance Checks (the "Guardrails Gauntlet")
- No personal data exposure
- No tactical details that could increase public risk
- Preserve chain-of-command and jurisdiction boundaries
- Require source attribution for policy claims
- Flag uncertainty instead of inventing facts

6) Define Success Criteria (the "Scoreboard")
- Accuracy to role and jurisdiction
- Policy alignment and public-service tone
- Actionability under real-world constraints
- Responsible handling of unknowns
- Clear, concise outputs for busy teams

7) Make It Replayable (the "Arcade Cabinet")
- For each prompt, add variants:
  - low/high urgency
  - weekday/weekend staffing
  - normal/limited budget
  - coordinated/siloed agency communications

8) Final Deliverable Shape
- 20–30 SLED prompts total
- Balanced across law enforcement, emergency management, records, and IT/security
- Each prompt includes: objective, constraints, expected output style, and scoring notes

Closing vibe:
Serious public service, playful testing energy. Think “mission control for counties,”
with clarity, safety, and community trust as the main characters.
